A MESSAGE
FROM MARTIN

“I care for the people Denton county JP Precinct 2, and I have a passion to serve. My involvement with our communities has helped me understand the problems people face and shaped me run for this position."

I’m running for Justice of the Peace because I believe our community deserves a fair, impartial, and principled voice in the courtroom someone who upholds the law with integrity and compassion. I’m committed to restoring confidence by ensuring that every individual is treated with dignity, regardless of background or status.

If I’m blessed to serve by God’s favor and the people’s trust As Justice of the Peace, I will bring clarity, consistency, and a heart for the people. Whether it’s resolving disputes, overseeing evictions, or officiating marriages, I will approach each case with the seriousness it deserves and the humanity it requires.

Family & Faith

Martin and his wife Sirsha have been married for 18 years and are blessed with three sons: Daniel Einstein, a 10th grader; Joseph Boanerges, 5th grader; and Peter Reinhard, 2nd grader. Martin comes from a legacy of faith his father served as a lifelong evangelist, and his grandfather was a missions pastor with the American Baptist Missions in India. Sirsha’s comes from a Lutheran background, adding to the family’s rich spiritual heritage. They are proud members of Prestonwood Baptist Church in Plano.

EXPERIENCE AND
COMMITMENT

As a Conservative Republican and lifelong Good Samaritan, I bring a principled, service-driven approach to the role of Justice of the Peace in Denton County. With experience in IT management (PMP certified) and business leadership, I’ve developed the problem solving skills and decision-making clarity essential for fair and efficient judicial service. My professional background also includes licensure as a real estate and mortgage officer, equipping me with firsthand insight into the disputes civil and property-related matters often brought before the court. These experiences, combined with my active role in social service leadership, reflect a life committed to both justice and community upliftment.

My advocacy extends beyond the courtroom. As the founder of the Texas Pastors & Leaders Group and a vocal supporter of the persecuted church, I’ve worked tirelessly to defend religious freedom and promote peace among interfaith organizations. I believe justice must be rooted in moral courage and compassion principles I’ve upheld through years of ministry, civic engagement, and public speaking. I’ve consistently used my voice to champion truth, dignity, and reconciliation.
